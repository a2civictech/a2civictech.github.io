Ann Arbor Civic Technology Meetup (a2civictech)

The Ann Arbor Civic Technology Meetup (a2civictech) meets at Ann Arbor City Hall during City of Ann Arbor public meetings and at other locations throughout Washtenaw County at other public meetings.

To RSVP for a future meeting, see our Meetup site. The next meeting is not yet planned; the location is to be determined. Join the Meetup group to get meeting locations and future dates for events. Ann Arbor City Council meetings are generally Mondays at 7:00 p.m., with exceptions for holidays.

The group meets to discuss technology and the city. Attendees include programmers, project managers, municipal and county IT managers, civic organizers, local politicians, and the occasional curious soul who finds the group on Meetup and decides to join in.

We use a Github issues tracker to identify open civic issues and to track projects for the group. To participate and contribute, you'll want a free Github account.

Follow occasional announcements on Twitter: @a2civictech.

In addition to monthly meetings, a2civictech supports the following ongoing online projects:

    Arborwiki, a local encyclopedia; part of the worldwide Localwiki project.
    The Ann Arbor Area Government Document Repository, an independent collection of public records related to local municipal governance hosted at the Ann Arbor District Library.
    #a2council, a bimonthly Twitter feed for followers of the Ann Arbor City Council; tune in the 1st and 3rd Mondays of most months.
