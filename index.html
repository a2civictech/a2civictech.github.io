Ann Arbor Civic Technology Meetup (a2civictech)

The Ann Arbor Civic Technology Meetup (a2civictech) meets on the 4th Monday of the month at a variety of locations in the Ann Arbor area.

The group is a local Brigade affiliate of the not-for-profit organization Code For America.

To RSVP for a future meeting, see our Meetup site. The next planned meeting is for 24 November 2014 at 7:00 p.m.; the location is to be determined. Join the Meetup group to get meeting locations and future dates for events.

The group meets to discuss technology and the city. Attendees include programmers, project managers, municipal and county IT managers, civic organizers, local politicians, and the occasional curious soul who finds the group on Meetup and decides to join in.

We use a Github issues tracker to identify open civic issues and to track projects for the group. To participate and contribute, you'll want a free Github account.

Follow occasional announcements on Twitter: @a2civictech.

Some sponsors of a2civictech include:

  • Code For America, for organizational and financial support

  • Menlo Innovations, for event hosting and support
  • Michigan Municipal League, for event hosting and support
  • Roos Roast, for event hosting and support
  • Workantile, for event hosting and support
  • Washtenaw County Government, for event hosting and support

In addition to monthly meetings, a2civictech supports the following ongoing online projects:

    Arborwiki, a local encyclopedia; part of the worldwide Localwiki project.
    The Ann Arbor Area Government Document Repository, an independent collection of public records related to local municipal governance.
    #a2council, a bimonthly Twitter feed for followers of the Ann Arbor City Council; tune in the 1st and 3rd Mondays of most months.
